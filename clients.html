• Apple continues to be a innovator in the hardware, software, and media industries. Its sales and revenues are proof of that fact. But to make sure those products and services translate into profits, their sales organization needs to be just as innovative.

    Before Mustard Grain implemented Apple’s internal sales training web application, there were no metrics on its channel sales training initiatives. We worked with Apple’s sales team to create a streamlined set of web-based sales tools. With the new system in place, the sales team was able to offer web-based training and assessment, which cut down on the test assessment costs, and allowed the team to determine the accuracy and efficiency of the training to yet further lower costs.

  • Cisco’s Flip line of personalized video cameras have revolutionized point-and-shoot video recording. FlipShare is Cisco’s video sharing service dedicated for Flip users to allow them to seamlessly share videos with family and friends.

    Cisco leveraged Mustard Grain’s expertise in scaling its FlipShare service. The first project added social networking aspects to their video sharing service. We helped introduce Cisco to Cassandra, which now powers their production comment service. The second project implemented usage data collection, processing, and reporting using Hadoop’s Chukwa project. As part of this work we fixed a few performance and scalability issues and added some enhancements, which have been contributed back to the community.

  • Adobe’s LiveCycle Mosaic module increases user productivity by providing personalized view of multiple applications in a single workspace. It promotes better decision-making by providing real-time, targeted, and contextual information.

    Adobe contacted Mustard Grain to develop the back-end services for LiveCycle Mosaic. One of the core design aspects was that the service be designed for Internet-scale collaboration. The back-end is composed of multiple, discrete services, each of which exposes a simple REST-based API. The initial server prototype was written in under two weeks, thanks to the productivity of the Grails framework. Over the course of the initial release, we migrated the various services to Java-based implementations, maintaining the API.

  • LinkedIn consistently scores within the top 50 most trafficked web sites on the Internet. One critical piece of the puzzle that allows LinkedIn to handle that traffic is its own open source NoSQL database, Voldemort.

    Mustard Grain was called on to help implement significant portions of Voldemort. The first project consisted of re-implementing the network protocol layer using an event-driven, non-blocking approach. The result is that Voldemort can now scale past its previous limit of a few hundred clients to support tens of thousands of simultaneous connected clients per node. We’ve also implemented a cloud-based performance testing solution, the failure detection subsystem, and added parallelism enhancements. As Voldemort is open source, all of the code has been contributed back to the community.

  • Karmasphere is a hot startup leading the charge to bring the power of Big Data to Analysts, Data Scientists, and Developers. The company is getting a lot of traction with their Hadoop development environment, Karmasphere Studio.

    Mustard Grain was brought in to extend their offerings to include an easy-to-use version of Hive, the open source, Hadoop-based data warehouse project. With our help, Karmasphere Analyst was born. Mustard Grain’s knowledge of Hadoop and open source allowed us to extend Hive, both as part of the Hive code base itself, as well as to provide a framework on top of Hive, including JDBC support. We are in the process of working to contribute significant changes back to the Hive community.

  • Everyone is talking about Big Data. SAP knows a little about the complexity that can arise from having so much data. The trick is how to make that data actionable and in a timely fashion. SAP’s BusinessObjects Text Analysis team is charged with turning all that unstructured data into insight.

    As you’d expect, extracting meaningful information from millions of documents is a pretty strenuous task. Mustard Grain worked with the Text Analysis team to enhance their system to work efficiently in a distributed fashion over a cluster of processing nodes. This allowed the cluster to process documents concurrently, report and retry failures, and implement statistical reporting. Mustard Grain then implemented a plugin-based workflow system atop the cluster that would allow non-technical users to get exactly the results they need.

  • Trintech is the leading global provider of integrated software solutions for finance. And by leading we mean that their customers include 50% of the Fortune 50. So a real-time financial application for companies with tens of thousands of employees brings some interesting challenges.

    Mustard Grain was asked to replace the application’s aging, custom caching framework and integrate their application with a rock-solid open source caching framework. We sat down with the team to really understand the nuances of their application. Based on the criteria they outlined, we researched, prototyped, and then proposed a number of open source solutions. After more rigorous testing and performance analysis, we settled on a solution which is still serving them well, several years later.
